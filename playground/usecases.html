1. Multi-Tenant / Role-Based B2B Search

In enterprise portals, different roles expect different results for the exact same query.

Problem: Standard vector DBs mix marketing, sales, and devops docs together. Creating separate databases per role is expensive and hard to maintain.
WarpVector Solution: Keep one single vector database. Apply a lightweight IntentAdapter matrix based on the user's role (e.g., Sales vs. DevOps) to instantly warp the space and surface relevant docs.

2. Fully Offline, On-Device AI Search

Ideal for privacy-first mobile apps, medical tools, or personal note-taking apps where data shouldn't leave the device.

Problem: Float32 embeddings consume massive RAM and bandwidth. You can't fit a 100,000-document vector database into a mobile browser cache.
WarpVector Solution: Apply QuantizationAdapter to compress vectors into Binary format (96.9% reduction). 100k vectors now fit in ~19MB, running sub-millisecond similarity search locally via WASM.

3. "Microscope" Search for Legal Contracts

When dealing with highly uniform text (like legal jargon or medical records), standard embeddings fail to differentiate nuances.

Problem: Anisotropy bias causes all legal clauses to have a similarity score of 0.98+ to the query. Top results are almost random.
WarpVector Solution: The WhiteningAdapter analyzes the dataset's principal components on the fly and stretches the vector space, acting like a microscope to amplify the actual semantic differences.
Contract Analyzer (Query: "Indemnification")

4. Serverless "Learns What You Like" Feed

Build TikTok or News aggregator style personalized feeds where the model learns from user clicks without expensive backend retraining.

Problem: Sending user interactions to the cloud to fine-tune a model per user is incredibly expensive, slow, and raises privacy concerns.
WarpVector Solution: Run InfoNCETrainer directly in the browser. Every swipe or "like" instantly fine-tunes a local 1MB transformation matrix, re-ranking the feed offline.

5. E-Commerce "Shop the Look" (Semantic Algebra)

Allow users to construct complex searches by adding or subtracting concepts intuitively, rather than relying on exact keyword matches.

Problem: A user has a photo of a "Red Jacket" but wants to find something similar that is "Vintage" and NOT "Modern". Standard image search can't handle logical modifiers.
WarpVector Solution: Use Vector Symbolic Architecture (VsaAdapter) to perform math at the edge: Vector(Image) + Vector(Vintage) - Vector(Modern).
